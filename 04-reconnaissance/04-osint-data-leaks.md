# Lesson 3 - OSINT: Data Leaks

## Status
- Planned

## Overview
- Data leaks are accidental or unauthorized exposures of information that can help an attacker understand a target, gain access, or increase credibility in social engineering.
- In an OSINT context, the focus is on identifying exposed data legally and using it to improve defensive understanding.

## Common Leak Sources
- Public code repositories containing secrets, tokens, or configuration files.
- Exposed backups, storage buckets, logs, documents, or database exports.
- Breach datasets that reveal email addresses, usernames, or reused credentials.
- Public collaboration platforms, paste sites, and archived web content.

## Why Leaks Matter
- Email addresses and naming conventions help map employees and internal structure.
- Credentials, API keys, or tokens may lead directly to unauthorized access if still valid.
- Internal documents can reveal technologies, business processes, vendors, and infrastructure details.

## What to Verify Carefully
- Whether the data is real, current, and relevant to the target.
- Whether access to the source is legal and within the engagement scope.
- Whether handling or storing the exposed data creates additional risk.

## Responsible Handling
- Minimize access to sensitive data and collect only what is necessary as evidence.
- Report exposed secrets or personal data through the approved engagement or disclosure channel.
- Avoid downloading large datasets or interacting with content beyond what is necessary to confirm exposure.

## Notes
- OSINT on leaks should improve security understanding, not turn into uncontrolled data collection.
