## What Risk Means in Cybersecurity
- Risk is the possibility that a threat will exploit a vulnerability and cause harm to the organization.
- The harm may be financial, operational, legal, reputational, or safety-related.

## Elements of Risk
- Asset: something valuable that must be protected.
- Threat: a source of possible harm.
- Vulnerability: a weakness that can be exploited.
- Impact: the damage caused if the event happens.
- Likelihood: how plausible or frequent the event is.

## Basic Risk Thinking
- A common simplified view is: risk depends on likelihood and impact.
- Higher likelihood and higher impact generally mean higher priority.

## Risk Treatment Options
- Avoid: stop the risky activity entirely.
- Reduce: apply controls that lower likelihood or impact.
- Transfer: shift part of the financial burden, for example through insurance or contracts.
- Accept: formally acknowledge the risk when treatment is not justified or feasible.

## Risk Management Process
- Identify important assets and business processes.
- Identify threats, vulnerabilities, and existing controls.
- Assess likelihood and impact.
- Prioritize risks.
- Choose treatment actions and owners.
- Reassess after controls are applied.

## Useful Artifacts
- Risk register listing each risk, owner, treatment, deadline, and status.
- Exception process for accepted risks.
- Review cycle so risks are revisited when systems, threats, or business priorities change.

## Notes
- Risk management is not about eliminating all risk. It is about making informed decisions and reducing unacceptable exposure.
