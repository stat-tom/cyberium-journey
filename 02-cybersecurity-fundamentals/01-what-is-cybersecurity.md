# Lesson 1 - What Is Cybersecurity?

## Status
- Completed

## Definition
- Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, applications, and data from unauthorized access, disruption, alteration, or destruction.
- Its goal is not only to block attacks, but also to keep the organization operating safely when incidents happen.

## What Needs Protection
- Devices such as laptops, phones, servers, routers, and industrial equipment.
- Software such as operating systems, business applications, cloud services, and APIs.
- Data such as credentials, personal information, financial records, intellectual property, and logs.
- Processes such as backups, access approvals, incident handling, and change management.
- People, because users and administrators are often the first line of defense and a common target.

## Main Security Goals
- Protect confidentiality so sensitive data is seen only by authorized people.
- Protect integrity so information and systems are not changed in an unauthorized way.
- Protect availability so systems and data remain accessible when needed.

## Why Cybersecurity Matters
- Cyber incidents can lead to financial loss, legal exposure, operational downtime, and reputational damage.
- Attackers target organizations of every size, not only large enterprises.
- Digital transformation increases the attack surface because more systems are connected to the internet, cloud platforms, and third parties.

## Key Areas of Cybersecurity
- Network security focuses on traffic control, segmentation, and secure communication.
- Application security reduces vulnerabilities in web, mobile, desktop, and API software.
- Endpoint security protects user devices and servers.
- Cloud security covers identities, configurations, logging, and workload protection in cloud environments.
- Identity and access management controls who can access what and under which conditions.
- Security operations monitor, detect, investigate, and respond to suspicious activity.

## Common Principles
- Least privilege means users and services should have only the access they need.
- Defense in depth means multiple layers of controls are used so one failure does not expose everything.
- Assume breach means designing as if an attacker may eventually gain a foothold.
- Security is a business process, not a one-time technical setup.

## Notes
- Cybersecurity is a balance between risk reduction, usability, cost, and business needs.
