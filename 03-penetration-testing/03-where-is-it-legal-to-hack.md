# Lesson 3 - Where Is It Legal to Hack?

## Status
- Planned

## Core Rule
- Hacking is legal only when there is explicit authorization from the system owner and the activity stays within the agreed scope.
- Technical ability does not create permission. Ownership and written approval do.

## Situations That Are Generally Legal
- Penetration testing performed under a signed contract or written rules of engagement.
- Security testing in personal lab environments that you own and control.
- Participation in bug bounty programs where the target and testing rules are clearly defined.
- Capture the Flag platforms and training labs built for legal practice.

## Situations That Are Usually Illegal or Risky
- Scanning, exploiting, or attempting access to systems without prior authorization.
- Testing assets that belong to a third party even if they are connected to an approved target.
- Exceeding the scope of a bug bounty or contract, for example by targeting forbidden domains or using prohibited techniques.
- Causing service disruption, data loss, or unauthorized data access outside the permitted rules.

## Why Written Scope Matters
- It defines which systems, dates, methods, and goals are approved.
- It protects both the tester and the client from misunderstandings.
- It establishes escalation contacts and incident procedures if something goes wrong.

## Good Operational Practices
- Keep copies of authorization, contracts, and program rules before testing begins.
- Document what you do and when you do it.
- Stop and escalate if you discover assets or conditions that appear outside scope.
- Treat collected data as sensitive and handle it according to the engagement rules.

## Notes
- In penetration testing, authorization is the boundary between professional security work and unlawful activity.
