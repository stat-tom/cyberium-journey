# Lesson 4 - Building a Hacker Mindset

## Status
- Planned

## What the Hacker Mindset Means
- A hacker mindset means thinking critically about how systems really behave, not just how they were intended to behave.
- It involves curiosity, persistence, structured experimentation, and a habit of questioning assumptions.

## Useful Ways of Thinking
- Break complex systems into smaller parts such as inputs, trust boundaries, identities, and dependencies.
- Look for assumptions developers or administrators may have made, especially around authentication, validation, and authorization.
- Ask how an attacker could abuse normal features instead of searching only for obvious technical bugs.
- Think in chains: several low-severity weaknesses together may create serious impact.

## Practical Habits
- Observe carefully before acting so you understand the target and reduce noise.
- Keep detailed notes, payloads, screenshots, and timelines during testing.
- Reproduce findings consistently instead of relying on one-off behavior.
- Learn enough about operating systems, networking, web applications, and scripting to investigate issues from multiple angles.

## Ethical Boundaries
- A professional tester stays within scope, minimizes harm, and protects client data.
- Creativity is important, but discipline matters just as much.
- The goal is to improve security, not to show off exploitation skill.

## Long-Term Development
- Study real incidents, writeups, and remediation patterns.
- Build small labs where you can safely test ideas and understand failure modes.
- Review findings after each exercise and ask what signals were missed or what assumptions slowed you down.

## Notes
- The most effective mindset in offensive security combines curiosity with method, evidence, and restraint.
